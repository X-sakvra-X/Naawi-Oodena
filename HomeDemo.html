The two main partners involved in developing Naawi-Oodena are the Treaty One Nations (T1N) and the Canada Lands Company.

The Treaty One Nations represent seven First Nations: Brokenhead, Long Plain, Peguis, Roseau River, Sagkeeng, Sandy Bay, and Swan Lake. They hold a 68% stake in the 160-acre site, which is now the largest urban reserve in Canada.

The Canada Lands Company, a federal Crown corporation, manages the remaining 32% of the site. Together, they signed a Comprehensive Settlement Agreement in 2019, paving the way for this transformative project.

It describes both a physical location and a profound abstract understanding which indicates the importance of the site to the Treaty One Nations. This signifies:

  • A return to the center of the ancestral land.
  • A reclamation of territory within a city, Winnipeg.
  • A vision of healing, resurgence for Indigenous people.
  • The converging point for economic, cultural and social development.

The Naawi-Oodena development site spans a total of 160 acres making it the largest urban reserve in Canada. This expansive area, formerly known as the Kapyong Barracks, is being transformed into a vibrant, mixed-use community.
